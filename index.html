Florida MCLE Requirements Florida Accredited Provider: 236059

Florida MCLE Deadline Florida State Bar groups attorneys into 1 of 3 compliance groups depending on your last name. The MCLE deadlines for these groups are:

  • Group 1 (A-G) Compliance Deadline: 02/01/2025 Reporting Deadline: Feb 1st, 2025

  • Group 2 (H-M) Compliance Deadline: 02/01/2025 Reporting Deadline: Feb 1st, 2025

  • Group 3 (N-Z) Compliance Deadline: 02/01/2025 Reporting Deadline: Feb 1st, 2025

Requirements 25 hours every 3 years, including 4 hours in Legal Ethics, 2 hours of Elimination of Bias (1 of which must be Implicit Bias), 2 hours of Competence (1 of which must be Prevention and Detection Competence and 1 may be Wellness Competence), 1 hour of Technology and the Practice of Law, and 1 hour of Civility in the Legal Profession.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many CLER hours are required?
Florida lawyers shall complete a minimum of 30 credit hours every 3 years. Three of the 30 credit hours must be in approved legal ethics, substance abuse, or mental illness awareness programs. Two of the 30 credit hours must be in approved professionalism program. The 2 hour professionalism course is only offered by the Florida Bar. Three of the 30 credit hours must be in approved technology programs.

10 Inventions That Changed the World

There is a large remote fire loss in Alaska that takes 1-2 days of travel once youre off the plane. Just as you arrive with your team, you get a text message that one of the parties will not be able to attend and wants to reschedule. What do you do?

This is where 3D scanning such as LiDAR and photogrammetry could save weeks of time and $1,000s for all parties involved. This is an actual case example pulled from the files of Andrew Bennet, a fire & explosion investigator. In this CLE, Andrew Bennet will show you how 3D scanning can be deployed in your cases, detail the difference between LiDAR & photogrammetry, present 2 case examples & 2 actual 3D models, address the limitations and illustrate how 3D scanning can be used in litigation to virtually walk juries through crime scenes, accident reconstructions and fire investigations. Additional topics: scene documentation, virtual inspections, spoliation, crime scene work, stitching, joint scene exams vs. solitary exams, discovery & alteration, admissibility and receiving the 3D model.

Andrew Bennett is Fire & Explosion investigator at Envista Forensics. He has more than 20 years of experience, conducting over 1600 fire and explosion investigations globally in both the public and private sectors.
